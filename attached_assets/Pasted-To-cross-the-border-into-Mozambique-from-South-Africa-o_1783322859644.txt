To cross the border into Mozambique from South Africa or eSwatini with a vehicle, you must present a specific set of personal, vehicle, and safety documents. Ensuring you have both the originals and certified copies will help prevent delays or fines at the border posts.👤 Personal DocumentsPassport: Must be valid for at least 30 days past your return date (though 6 months is highly recommended) and contain at least 2–3 blank pages.Visas: Citizens of South Africa and other SADC countries do not require a tourist visa for stays up to 30 days. Non-SADC passport holders must apply for an eVisa beforehand via the official government portal.Driver's License: A valid national driver's license from your home country (such as a South African or eSwatini license) is legally recognized. An International Driving Permit (IDP) is recommended but not legally compulsory.Travel with Minors: If traveling with children under 18 from South Africa, you must carry their Unabridged Birth Certificates. If only one parent is traveling, a signed Parental Consent Affidavit is required.🚗 Vehicle DocumentsVehicle Registration Papers: You must present the original registration document or a certified copy.Letter of Authority (Border Letter):If the vehicle is financed, you must present a formal letter from your bank granting permission to take the car into Mozambique for your specific travel dates.If the vehicle is not in your name, you need a signed affidavit or letter of authority from the registered owner giving you permission to drive it across the border.Temporary Import Permit (TIP): Issued for free at the Mozambican customs desk. You will fill out the required vehicle particulars form at the border to receive it.Compulsory Third-Party Insurance: Fully comprehensive insurance from your home country is not legally sufficient on its own. You must purchase Mozambican third-party road insurance, which is available online beforehand or from official insurance booths right outside the border post.🧰 Compulsory Vehicle Safety GearMozambican traffic police strictly enforce compliance with the following safety equipment:Two Red Warning Triangles: Compulsory for all vehicles.Reflective Jackets: At least two high-visibility reflective vests must be kept inside the cabin of the car.Fire Extinguisher: A valid, functioning fire extinguisher must be kept in the vehicle.Nationality Sticker: If driving a South African vehicle, a "ZA" sticker must be affixed to the rear bumper.Towing Sticker: If you are towing a boat, trailer, or caravan, you must stick a blue-and-yellow triangle sticker on the front of your vehicle and on the rear of the towed item
