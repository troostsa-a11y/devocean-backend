Overview

Ponta do Ouro sits directly in the migration corridor of the East African humpback whale population. An estimated 30,000 humpback whales pass through Mozambican waters July–November, making this one of the most reliable whale watching destinations in the Southern Hemisphere. Marine biologist-led seafari boats depart the village beach daily during peak season.

Year-round, the same boats encounter the resident bottlenose dolphin pods, and October–March adds whale sharks and nesting sea turtles to the mix. Manta rays congregate December–May.

What to See & When

Humpback Whales — Jul to Nov

Peak migration July–October. Behaviours: breaching (full body out of water), tail-slapping, spy-hopping, and approach behaviour where whales investigate the boat. Calf sightings common late August–October on the return southward journey.

Whale Sharks — Oct to Mar

Largest fish on earth — seasonal visitors feeding on bait fish concentrations in warmer months. Snorkel encounters possible when sighted (not scuba). Sightings not guaranteed.

Bottlenose Dolphins — Year-round

200+ resident Indo-Pacific bottlenose dolphins present throughout the year. Often encountered en route to whale grounds. Frequently bowride alongside the boat.

Manta Rays — Dec to May

Reef and oceanic manta rays present in warmer months. Often encountered at cleaning stations and feeding on surface plankton.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • How likely is it to see humpback whales?
    During peak season (July–October), sighting rates are very high — operators run daily trips and know the migration routes well. Whales are large, surface-active animals; in peak season multiple sightings per trip are common. Outside July–November, whale sightings are not expected.
  • Do seafari trips include snorkelling?
    Most operators combine whale watching with a reef snorkelling stop. Snorkel equipment (mask, fins, snorkel) is included in the trip price. Swimming ability is required for the snorkelling portion but whale watching alone requires no special skills.
  • Is seasickness a concern on seafaris?
    Trips depart in the morning when seas are typically calmest. Conditions in June–September (winter) are generally the most stable. Bring motion sickness medication if you are prone. Operators may reschedule trips if sea conditions are unsafe.
