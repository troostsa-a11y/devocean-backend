Overview

The Ponta do Ouro Partial Marine Reserve is one of Southern Africa's most biodiverse dive destinations. Crystal-clear Indian Ocean water, 20+ named dive sites across a wide depth range, and 19 shark species make this a bucket-list destination for divers of all experience levels — from beginners on shallow reef dives to experienced divers chasing pelagics at 40+ metres.

Multiple PADI-certified dive centres operate from the village, offering everything from introductory dives and Open Water courses to specialist shark and technical diving. Equipment rental is included in all dive prices. Marine park fees are covered by operators.

Seasonal Highlights

November – April

Warm water (26–29°C). Peak season for bull sharks and tiger sharks at Pinnacles. Whale shark sightings October–March.

April – October

Great hammerhead season. Best water visibility (20–30 m) in May–August (cooler, 22–24°C). Humpback whales July–November.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Do I need a PADI certification to dive at Ponta do Ouro?
    Yes — a PADI Open Water (or equivalent NAUI/SSI) certification is required for guided dives. Multiple operators on-site offer the PADI Open Water course for $350–400 USD, including all equipment. Minimum age is 10 years (Junior Open Water). Experienced divers should bring their certification card.
  • What is the water temperature for diving at Ponta do Ouro?
    Water temperature ranges from 22°C (July–August, dry season) to 28–29°C (January–February, summer). A 3 mm wetsuit is recommended year-round — surface conditions can be cooler than the water, especially on morning boat trips in winter.
  • Can you dive with whale sharks at Ponta do Ouro?
    Yes — whale sharks are seasonal visitors October–March. Sightings are more common when bait fish and plankton are present in warmer water. Operators running dive trips during this season will divert to whale shark encounters when one is spotted. These are snorkel/freedive encounters rather than scuba dives due to the sharks' surface behaviour.
  • How many dives per day can you do at Ponta do Ouro?
    Most operators run two-dive morning trips (3–4 hours total). Full-day three-dive trips are also available (6–8 hours). Surface intervals are observed between dives. Most operators also offer afternoon dives subject to conditions.
