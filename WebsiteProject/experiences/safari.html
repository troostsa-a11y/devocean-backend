Overview

Maputo National Park (formerly Maputo Special Reserve) is one of Mozambique's most accessible wildlife destinations — just 30 km north of Ponta do Ouro. Inscribed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2025 as part of the Lubombo Transfrontier Conservation Area, the 1,718 km² park is often described as "where the Okavango meets the Indian Ocean" for its dramatic mosaic of savanna, coastal dunes, wetlands and mangroves.

The park's 450–500 coastal elephants are the star attraction — a unique population adapted to sandy coastal terrain. Day trips depart Ponta do Ouro early morning by 4x4, allowing 5–6 hours of guided game driving before returning in the late afternoon.

Wildlife at Maputo National Park

Megafauna

  • Elephants — 450–500 coastal population
  • Hippos — around lakes and wetlands
  • Giraffes — frequently sighted
  • Zebras — common, often in herds
  • Crocodiles — Maputo Bay and rivers

Predators

  • Leopard — shy, forest edges
  • Cheetah — reintroduced 2023–24
  • Spotted hyena — reintroduced 2023–24

Antelope

  • Nyala, kudu, eland, impala
  • Reedbuck, waterbuck, red duiker

Birds

  • 300+ species recorded
  • Flamingos in coastal lagoons
  • Raptors, wading birds
  • Best Oct–Mar for migrant species

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Do I need a 4x4 to visit Maputo National Park?
    Yes — all tracks inside the park are thick sand. Day trip operators provide a 4x4 vehicle with driver/guide from Ponta do Ouro. Self-drive visitors also need a capable 4x4 (a standard sedan or soft-roader is not suitable). The drive from Ponta do Ouro to the park entrance takes approximately 30–45 minutes.
  • Is the safari suitable for children?
    Yes — game drives are vehicle-based so no fitness is required. Children of all ages are welcome. USB charging is available in most vehicles. Sun protection, neutral-coloured clothing (khaki, beige, olive — not bright colours) and insect repellent are recommended for all ages.
  • Can you do a multi-day camping safari from Ponta do Ouro?
    Yes — 2–3 day camping safaris inside Maputo National Park start from approximately $200 USD per person. Contact DEVOCEAN Lodge or operators Maputo National Park (reservas@parquemaputo.gov.mz) or Transportes Mulungo for camping safari packages.
