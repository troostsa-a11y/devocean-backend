Overview

Ponta do Ouro's right-hand point break is one of Southern Africa's most rewarding uncrowded waves — when the swell and wind align, rides of 100–200 m are typical, and the point can fire up to 1 km in exceptional conditions. The warm Indian Ocean water (22–28°C year-round) and uncrowded lineups make this a genuine alternative to better-known South African surf destinations.

Surf lessons and board rental are available from Brasukas Bar & Surf on the beach. Softboards, shortboards and funboards are available. Dolphins frequently ride the same wave as surfers — one of Ponta do Ouro's most celebrated experiences.

Surf Spots

Intermediate – Advanced

Ponta do Ouro Point

The main event. Classic right-hander over rock and sand. 100–200m rides, up to 1km on a firing south swell with west wind. Rips along the point — respect the ocean.

Beginner – Intermediate

Beach Breaks

Mellow sandy beach breaks adjacent to the point. Softer, more forgiving waves ideal for learning. Safe sandy bottom.

All levels

Ponta Malongane (8 km north)

Alternative spot with a different swell window and consistently less crowded. Worth the drive when the point is onshore.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Do I need to bring my own surfboard to Ponta do Ouro?
    No — boards are available for hire from Brasukas Bar & Surf. However, board stock is limited. Experienced surfers travelling with specific board preferences should bring their own. Bring backup fins and a spare leash — surf shops in the village have minimal stock.
  • Do I need a wetsuit to surf in Ponta do Ouro?
    Water temperature is 22–28°C year-round so a full wetsuit is rarely needed. Boardshorts or a 1–2mm rashguard suit most of the year. In June–August, morning offshore winds can feel cold — a 2–3mm shorty wetsuit provides comfort. Wetsuits are available from the surf school if needed.
  • Is the point break dangerous for beginners?
    The main point break has significant rips and a rock/sand bottom — it is suitable for intermediate to advanced surfers only. Beginners should stick to the beach breaks under surf school supervision. Surf lessons always start on the beach breaks. Always check conditions with locals before paddling out for the first time.
  • When is the best time of year to surf Ponta do Ouro?
    The point works year-round when conditions align. South Africa's south coast and the Mozambique Channel generate south swells throughout the year. The most consistent windows tend to align with winter swells (May–September) but summer can also produce good conditions. Check Surfline or Magicseaweed for the Ponta do Ouro forecast before your trip.
