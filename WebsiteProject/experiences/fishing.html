Overview

The Mozambique Channel is one of the world's premier big-game fishing grounds. Deep water is accessed within minutes of the beach launch at Ponta do Ouro, and the warm Indian Ocean temperatures (22–28°C) concentrate an exceptional range of pelagic species year-round. Expert local skippers with 20+ years of experience operate from the village.

The characteristic beach launch — riding a rubber duck through the surf — is part of the Ponta do Ouro fishing experience. Expect to get wet on the way out and the way back.

October – January (peak)

Premier billfish target. Trolling lures and live bait. Catch and release strongly encouraged.

Sailfish

Prime season. Trolling and fly fishing. Aggressive surface strikes common.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is included in a fishing charter at Ponta do Ouro?
    All fishing tackle and equipment, bait and lures, fishing licences and permits, an experienced skipper (20+ years local experience), fish cleaning and filleting, vacuum packaging for transport, and drinks and snacks on most operators. Safety equipment including life jackets is standard.
  • Do I need fishing experience to join a charter?
    No — skippers and mates will assist beginners throughout the trip. Most charters accommodate 4–6 anglers. A maximum of 6 anglers per boat is typical. Seasickness medication is recommended if you are prone to motion sickness on open water.
  • What should I bring for beach fishing?
    Bring your own gear — fishing tackle shops in Ponta do Ouro have very limited stock. Good shore fishing spots include the rocky point at the south end of the main beach (deepwater shore casts) and Ponta Mamoli 8 km north. Dawn and dusk produce the best results. Artificial lures (pink dusters, redeye, chokka) work well for shore species.
