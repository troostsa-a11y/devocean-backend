Overview

The Ponta do Ouro Partial Marine Reserve is home to over 200 resident Indo-Pacific bottlenose dolphins — not a visiting population but a permanent, catalogued community that has been studied since 1995. Two licensed operators run structured ethical encounters where dolphins choose to engage entirely on their own terms.

Trips include an educational briefing, boat transfer to the dolphins, in-water snorkelling facilitation, and often incidental encounters with whale sharks (October–March) and humpback whales (July–November).

Ethical Guidelines Strictly enforced

No touching

Strict no-contact policy. Dolphins remain wild and free at all times.

No chasing

Encounters happen entirely on the dolphins' terms. Facilitators never pursue.

No feeding

Wild dolphins hunt naturally. No artificial feeding under any circumstances.

Research-based

Data collected on every trip supports marine conservation. Dolphin Encountours has catalogued 250+ individuals since 1995.

Seasonal Calendar

January – May (Peak)

Best dolphin encounter rates. Calmest seas in early morning. Water 26–28°C. Occasional whale shark sightings through March.

June – September

Dolphins year-round. Cooler water (22–24°C). Humpback whales present July–November — multi-species trips possible.

October – December

Whale sharks return October. Sea turtles nesting on beaches. Good conditions resume after winter.

Year-round

Dolphins are permanent residents — no season is "bad." Early morning departures offer calmest sea conditions throughout the year.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Are dolphin sightings guaranteed?
    Sightings are not guaranteed — these are wild animals. However, with 200+ resident dolphins and experienced facilitators who know their ranges, encounter rates are very high. Operators will inform you honestly if conditions are poor on a given day.
  • How many people are on each dolphin trip?
    Groups are capped at a maximum of 12 people per trip. Only 2 operators hold permits — book early, especially December–January and during school holidays.
  • Is snorkel equipment provided?
    Yes — mask, fins, snorkel and wetsuit (if needed) are all included in the trip price. A light refreshment and marine park fees are also included. Bring sunscreen, water and a towel.
  • What species of dolphin are in Ponta do Ouro?
    Indo-Pacific bottlenose dolphins (Tursiops aduncus) — a distinct species from the common bottlenose dolphin found in temperate waters. The population is genetically isolated and has been individually catalogued. Spinner dolphins and common dolphins are occasional visitors.
